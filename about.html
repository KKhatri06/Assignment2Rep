About Me

Who I Am

I’m a Mechanical Engineering student at the University of Aberdeen. Ever since I was young, I’ve enjoyed solving problems and the sense of satisfaction that comes from figuring things out. In school, I found myself naturally drawn to engineering science and physics, and Mechanical Engineering felt like the perfect fit. It’s a broad discipline with many different fields and career paths, and I liked the idea of studying something that keeps my options open while still challenging me academically.

My Engineering Interests

Over time, I discovered that what excites me most is the design side of engineering. I enjoy working with CAD tools like SolidWorks and exploring how components and systems come together. I’m especially interested in becoming a design-focused engineer — someone who creates, models, and improves mechanical systems. I also like the idea of running simulations and analysing how machines behave under different conditions using engineering software. Subjects like fluid mechanics and thermodynamics have also been areas I’ve really enjoyed.

Skills I'm Developing

I’m proud of the technical skills I’ve developed so far, especially learning how to code. Even though I’m still at a beginner level, I can already see how valuable programming is for modern engineering, and I’m motivated to keep improving. I’m also proud of learning MATLAB this year — it’s a powerful tool for modelling, analysing data, and solving engineering problems, and I know it will be extremely useful in my future career. Gaining confidence with these tools has shown me that I can pick up new skills quickly when I put in the effort.

Hobbies & Interests

Outside of engineering, I like staying active and spending time with friends. Volleyball is one of my favourite sports — I enjoy the teamwork, competitiveness, and energy of the game. When I’m not playing sports, I like relaxing by playing games, especially Rainbow Six Siege. I also enjoy going on random drives with friends, exploring new places, and just having a good time. These things help me switch off from studying and keep a good balance in my life.

What This Website Shows

This website is part of my coursework and includes an introduction about me, more detailed information about my interests and studies, and a real-time chat application I built using Node.js and Socket.IO. It’s a small project, but it reflects my curiosity and willingness to learn new skills.
